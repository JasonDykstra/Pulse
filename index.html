Title Screen

Earth is ablaze. Unstoppable wildfires, global warming, and accelerated volcanism have shrunk the habitable range of the planet to narrow bands below the poles. With little more than a century left, the world's greatest scientists met at a secret base in the Canadian tundra to devise an escape. The result was Pulse, a long-range space probe laden with the atmospheric ingredients to terraform an Earth-like planet for human habitation. The team finished work in record time and, under the eyes of billions, sent the last hope of humankind soaring through the burning atmosphere.

And so the probe wandered, a lone beacon of life in the endless dark. Generations of technicians monitored the instruments in ever-decreasing numbers, but to no avail. Finally, ninety-five years after liftoff, a midnight watchman noticed a repeating signal pattern wink onto the faded dashboard. Snatching up his crumbling codebooks, he read late into the morning, until, with an ecstatic scream, he realized that these were radio waves - an incontrovertible sign of intelligent life.

Pinpointing the origin of the signals, the probe arrived at an uncharted exoplanet in the furthest reaches of the galaxy. Suddenly, the auditory transponders began to emit a scarcely-believable sound - that of music, crashing and howling in familiar tones that rose to a surreal climax with the craft's approach. The planet, similar in size and surface temperature to Earth, was perfectly suited for terraforming; only the upper atmosphere, whose roiling winds and asteroid belts appeared nearly impassable, stood between humanity and survival. The project leaders, less than a decade from annihilation, were determined to try. With the fate of an entire species in the balance, the aging vessel dropped toward the surface, and into the storm...
